# Quick Start: Discovering Keyboard Shortcuts 🚀

## For Users: How to Find Keyboard Shortcuts

You don't need to read a manual! Just **hover your mouse over buttons** and tooltips will show you the shortcuts.

---

## 🎯 Try It Now!

### Step 1: Open Any Dialog
- Click "Sign/Crypt" or "TLS" button
- Or open Partner Configuration
- Or open About dialog

### Step 2: Hover Over Buttons
Move your mouse over any button and wait ~0.5 seconds.

### Step 3: See the Tooltip
A small tooltip will appear showing the keyboard shortcut!

**Example:**
```
┌─────────┐
│   OK    │ ← Hover here
└─────────┘
     ↓
┌──────────────────┐
│ Keyboard: ENTER  │ ← Tooltip appears!
└──────────────────┘
```

### Step 4: Try the Shortcut
Press the key shown in the tooltip!

---

## ⌨️ Common Shortcuts You'll Discover

| When You Hover... | You'll See... | Try Pressing... |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| OK button | "Keyboard: ENTER" | **ENTER** key |
| Cancel button | "Keyboard: ESC" | **ESC** key |
| Any button | Platform-specific tip | Keys shown in tooltip |
| Save button | "Cmd+S" or "Ctrl+S" | **Cmd+S** (Mac) or **Ctrl+S** (Win) |

---

## 🎨 Universal Dialog Shortcuts

These work in **all dialogs**:

### On Mac:
- **ENTER** - Confirm/OK
- **ESC** - Cancel/Close  
- **Cmd+W** - Close dialog (like Safari, Finder)

### On Windows/Linux:
- **ENTER** - Confirm/OK
- **ESC** - Cancel/Close
- **Ctrl+W** - Close dialog

---

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Tip 1: Hover While Learning
When you're new, hover over buttons to see shortcuts. After a few times, you'll remember them!

### Tip 2: Look for Patterns
- Most "Confirm" actions use **ENTER**
- Most "Cancel" actions use **ESC**
- Most "Save" actions use **Cmd/Ctrl+S**
- Most "Print" actions use **Cmd/Ctrl+P**

### Tip 3: Explore!
Try hovering over different buttons in different dialogs. You might discover shortcuts you didn't know existed!

## ❓ FAQ

### Q: Why don't I see tooltips?
**A:** Wait 0.5 seconds after hovering. If still no tooltip, ensure tooltips are enabled in your OS settings.

### Q: The shortcut shows "Cmd" but I'm on Windows?
**A:** This shouldn't happen. The app auto-detects your platform. Please report this bug!

### Q: Can I customize the shortcuts?
**A:** Current version uses standard shortcuts. Custom shortcuts may be added in future versions.

### Q: Do shortcuts work when button is disabled?
**A:** No, shortcuts only work when the button is enabled (clickable).

### Q: Will this slow down my computer?
**A:** No! Tooltips use minimal resources and only appear when you hover.
